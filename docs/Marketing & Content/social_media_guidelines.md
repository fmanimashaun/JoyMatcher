## 1. Purpose & Philosophy

### 1.1 Why Social Media Matters

Social media is a critical channel for JoyMatcher to:
1. **Build brand awareness** among marriage-minded professionals
2. **Share success stories** that demonstrate platform effectiveness
3. **Educate users** on features, relationship advice, and cultural topics
4. **Engage community** and foster conversation around serious relationships
5. **Drive acquisition** through organic and paid content
6. **Position premium brand** that's sophisticated, not casual

### 1.2 Social Media is NOT

- ❌ Entertainment-focused (we're not trying to go viral with memes)
- ❌ Overly promotional (constant "Sign up now!" posts)
- ❌ Casual or playful (like Tinder or Bumble)
- ❌ Trend-chasing without brand alignment
- ❌ Generic dating advice (we're marriage-focused)

### 1.3 Social Media IS

- ✅ Educational and trust-building
- ✅ Sophisticated and aspirational
- ✅ Community-oriented and supportive
- ✅ Success story-driven (social proof)
- ✅ Culturally inclusive (especially African diaspora)
- ✅ Premium positioning

## 3. Content Creation Guidelines

### 3.1 Visual Identity

**Brand Colors:**
- Primary: Deep purple (#4D0052) to coral (#F16A6F) gradient
- Use in graphics, overlays, and branded elements
- Maintain consistency across all platforms

**Typography:**
- Primary Font: Georgia (serif, editorial, sophisticated)
- Use in quote graphics and branded templates
- Avoid playful fonts (Comic Sans, handwritten styles)

**Photography Style:**
- Professional, aspirational, diverse
- Real couples (success stories) > stock photos
- Warm, editorial color grading
- Generous negative space
- No overly posed or cheesy images

**Graphic Templates:**
- Create Canva templates for:
  - Success story announcements
  - Quote cards
  - Carousel posts (educational content)
  - Stat graphics
  - Tier explainers
- Use consistent layout and branding

**Video Style:**
- Natural, unscripted testimonials
- Soft background music (not distracting)
- Branded lower-thirds (name, location)
- High-quality audio (clear, no background noise)
- 16:9 (landscape) for YouTube/LinkedIn, 9:16 (vertical) for Instagram/TikTok

### 3.2 Copy Guidelines

**Tone:**
- Warm but professional (see Content Strategy doc)
- Sophisticated, not casual or slangy
- Encouraging and supportive, not preachy

**Length:**
- **Instagram Caption:** 150-300 words (first 125 chars are visible before "more")
- **Facebook Post:** 200-400 words (longer narratives okay)
- **LinkedIn Post:** 150-250 words (or long-form articles 800-1200 words)
- **X/Twitter:** 200-280 characters (leave room for retweets with comments)

**Emoji Usage:**
- Use sparingly and intentionally
- Acceptable: 💜 (brand color), 🌍 (diaspora), 💍 (engagement), ✨ (premium)
- Avoid: 😂 (too casual), 🔥 (too playful), ❤️ (prefer 💜 for brand consistency)

**Hashtags:**
- Instagram: 15-20 (in first comment to keep caption clean)
- Facebook: 3-5 (lower impact, but still useful)
- LinkedIn: 3-5
- X/Twitter: 2-3 (integrated into copy)

**CTAs:**
- Every post should have a clear call-to-action
- Examples: "Link in bio to join," "Read the full story on our blog," "Tag someone who's marriage-minded"
- Avoid aggressive CTAs: "Sign up NOW!" → "Ready to find your person? Link in bio."

### 3.3 Content Calendar Planning

**Planning Cycle:** Monthly (with weekly adjustments)

**Process:**
1. **Month Planning (Last week of previous month)**
   - Social Media Manager drafts calendar
   - Reviews blog posts, success stories, and campaigns planned for month
   - Identifies key dates (holidays, awareness days)
   - Assigns content pillars to specific days
   - Marketing team reviews and approves

2. **Weekly Scheduling (Monday mornings)**
   - Finalize copy and graphics for the week
   - Schedule posts using tool (Buffer, Hootsuite, Later)
   - Leave 20-30% flexibility for real-time content

3. **Daily Monitoring**
   - Respond to comments and DMs
   - Share Stories (Instagram, Facebook)
   - Engage with relevant hashtags and accounts
   - Monitor brand mentions

**Content Calendar Tool:** Airtable, Notion, Google Sheets, or native scheduling tool

**Calendar Columns:**
- Date & Time
- Platform
- Content Pillar (Success Story, Educational, etc.)
- Post Type (Feed, Reel, Story, etc.)
- Copy (full caption)
- Visual (link to graphic/video)
- Hashtags
- CTA
- Status (Drafted / Scheduled / Published)
- Performance Notes (engagement, reach)

### 3.4 Content Batching

**Efficiency Strategy:**
- Create 2-4 weeks of content in advance
- Batch similar tasks (all captions in one session, all graphics in another)
- Maintain flexibility for real-time content (news, trending topics, user engagement)

**Batching Schedule:**
- **Week 1 of Month:** Plan and outline all content for month
- **Week 2:** Design graphics and edit videos
- **Week 3:** Write all captions
- **Week 4:** Schedule content, review performance from previous month

## 5. Engagement & Community Management

### 5.1 Response Time

**Goal:** Respond to all comments and DMs within 2 hours during business hours (9am-6pm EST/GMT).

**Off-hours:** Set auto-reply in DMs:
```
"Thanks for reaching out! We're currently offline but will respond within 24 hours. For urgent inquiries, email support@joymatcher.com."
```

### 5.2 Comment Response Guidelines

**Positive Comments** ("This is beautiful!" "Congratulations!" "Goals!"):
- Like the comment
- Respond warmly:
  - "Thank you! We're so proud of Chioma and Kwame 💜"
  - "We love seeing our members find their person! Ready to start your journey?"

**Questions** ("How does this work?" "Is JoyMatcher available in [city]?"):
- Answer directly and helpfully
- Provide link if relevant
- Example:
  - "Great question! JoyMatcher uses a tier system where you unlock deeper compatibility by completing tiers yourself. Learn more: [link]"
  - "Yes, we're available in [city]! Join us: [link]"

**Negative Comments** (Criticism, skepticism):
- Respond professionally, not defensively
- Acknowledge and offer to help
- Take heated discussions to DMs
- Example:
  - "We appreciate your feedback. We'd love to hear more about your concerns—please DM us so we can help."
  - "We understand online dating can be frustrating. JoyMatcher is different—we're built for marriage, not casual dating. Learn more: [link]"

**Spam or Abusive Comments**:
- Delete and block
- Do NOT engage

### 5.3 DM Management

**Inquiries:**
- Answer common questions (pricing, features, availability)
- Provide links to website, blog, or support
- If complex, direct to customer support email

**Success Stories:**
- If user shares success story, thank them and ask for consent to feature
- Send formal testimonial survey link (see Testimonials System doc)

**Complaints:**
- Apologize and empathize
- Offer to escalate to support team
- Do NOT argue or dismiss

**Spam:**
- Ignore and delete
- Block if persistent

### 5.4 Social Listening

**Monitor for:**
- Brand mentions (@joymatcher, "JoyMatcher")
- Competitor mentions (for market insights)
- Industry keywords ("marriage-minded," "serious dating," "diaspora dating")
- Success stories shared by users (repost with permission)

**Tools:**
- Native platform search
- Mention, Brand24, or Hootsuite (paid tools)
- Google Alerts for press mentions

**Action:**
- Respond to mentions (thank users, address concerns)
- Engage with relevant conversations (add value, not spam)
- Track sentiment (positive, neutral, negative)

## 6. Paid Social Media Strategy

### 6.1 Advertising Objectives

**Primary Goals:**
1. **Brand Awareness:** Reach marriage-minded professionals
2. **Website Traffic:** Drive to homepage, blog, success stories
3. **Conversions:** Sign-ups and Premium upgrades

**Secondary Goals:**
4. **Engagement:** Grow followers, increase post engagement
5. **Retargeting:** Re-engage website visitors who didn't sign up

### 6.2 Platform Selection

**Instagram/Facebook Ads (Primary):**
- Best for visual storytelling (success stories, lifestyle)
- Strong targeting (interests, behaviors, demographics)
- Budget: 60-70% of total paid social budget

**LinkedIn Ads (Secondary):**
- Best for professional targeting (job titles, industries)
- Higher CPM but better quality for Professional segment
- Budget: 20-30% of total paid social budget

**X/Twitter Ads (Test):**
- Good for thought leadership and cultural conversations
- Budget: 10% of total paid social budget

### 6.3 Targeting Strategy

**Core Audience:**
- Age: 28-45
- Location:
  - **Diaspora cities:** Toronto, London, New York, Atlanta, Houston, Paris, Washington DC, Chicago
  - **Home markets:** Lagos, Accra, Nairobi, Johannesburg, Kampala
- Education: Bachelor's degree or higher
- Job Titles: Manager, Director, Executive, Consultant, Entrepreneur, Doctor, Lawyer, Engineer
- Interests: Career development, African culture, travel, weddings, relationships, personal development

**Lookalike Audiences:**
- Based on existing users (email list, Premium users, engaged users)
- 1% lookalike (most similar) for conversion campaigns
- 3-5% lookalike (broader) for awareness campaigns

**Retargeting Audiences:**
- Website visitors (last 30 days)
- Blog readers (last 30 days)
- Video viewers (75%+ completion)
- Social media engagers (liked, commented, shared)
- Email list non-converters

### 6.4 Ad Creative Best Practices

**Success Story Ads (Best Performers):**
- Use real couple photos (with consent)
- Carousel format (tell the story in 3-5 slides)
- Authentic testimonials (direct quotes)
- Clear CTA: "Find Your Person"
- A/B test: Photo vs. video

**Feature Explainer Ads:**
- Highlight unique features (tier system, VIP service)
- Use diagrams or animations
- Address pain points: "Tired of casual dating?"
- CTA: "Learn How It Works"

**Brand Awareness Ads:**
- Aspirational lifestyle imagery
- Mission-driven messaging: "Built for Marriage-Minded Professionals"
- No hard CTA (just "Learn More")

**Retargeting Ads:**
- Direct CTA: "Complete Your Profile," "Upgrade to Premium"
- Urgency (gentle): "Your Person is Waiting"
- Offer (if applicable): "First Month Free" (test carefully to avoid cheapening brand)

### 6.5 Budget Allocation

**Monthly Paid Social Budget:** $3,000-10,000 (adjust based on stage and goals)

**Breakdown:**
- **60% Conversion Campaigns** (Sign-ups, Premium upgrades)
  - Success story ads
  - Feature ads
  - Retargeting ads
- **30% Awareness Campaigns** (Reach new audiences)
  - Brand messaging
  - Educational content
  - Lookalike audiences
- **10% Testing** (New creatives, audiences, platforms)
  - A/B tests
  - New platform exploration (TikTok, X/Twitter)

**Performance Benchmarks:**
- **CPM (Cost per 1000 impressions):** $5-15
- **CPC (Cost per click):** $0.50-2.00
- **CTR (Click-through rate):** 1-3%
- **CPA (Cost per acquisition):** $20-50 (sign-up), $100-200 (Premium conversion)

(Adjust based on market and competition)

### 6.6 A/B Testing Strategy

**Test Variables:**
- Ad creative (image vs. video, different couples, different angles)
- Copy (long vs. short, different hooks)
- CTA (Join Now vs. Learn More vs. Find Your Person)
- Audience (different demographics, interests, behaviors)

**Testing Process:**
1. Run two variations simultaneously
2. Minimum budget: $100 per variation
3. Minimum duration: 7 days
4. Analyze: Which has better CTR, CPA, ROAS?
5. Scale winner, iterate loser or kill

## 8. Crisis Management & Negative Feedback

### 8.1 Crisis Scenarios

**Scenario 1: User Complaint Goes Viral**
- User posts negative experience publicly (e.g., "JoyMatcher scammed me!")

**Response:**
1. Respond quickly (within 1 hour if during business hours)
2. Acknowledge and empathize: "We're sorry to hear about your experience. This isn't the standard we hold ourselves to."
3. Take conversation offline: "Please DM us your account details so we can investigate and make this right."
4. Resolve privately: Refund, account review, apology
5. Follow up publicly (optional): "We've reached out to [User] and are working to resolve this. Thank you for your patience."

**Scenario 2: Feature Criticism**
- Users complain about pricing, tier system, or specific feature

**Response:**
1. Acknowledge feedback: "We appreciate your feedback. Here's why we built it this way..."
2. Explain rationale (link to blog post or explainer)
3. Invite further conversation: "We're always listening. DM us if you have more thoughts."
4. Do NOT: Get defensive, argue in comments, dismiss concerns

**Scenario 3: Cultural Insensitivity Accusation**
- User claims post or content is culturally insensitive or offensive

**Response:**
1. Take seriously: "Thank you for bringing this to our attention."
2. Investigate internally: Is the concern valid?
3. If valid: Apologize publicly, remove content, explain corrective action
4. If misunderstanding: Clarify intent respectfully, still acknowledge hurt feelings
5. Learn and improve: Update guidelines to prevent recurrence

**Scenario 4: Data Breach or Security Concern**
- User reports security issue or fears data compromise

**Response:**
1. Escalate immediately to technical and legal teams
2. Do NOT downplay or dismiss
3. Respond publicly (if public claim): "We take security seriously. We're investigating this claim and will update as soon as possible."
4. Resolve and communicate: Transparent update once issue is confirmed/resolved
5. Offer support: "If you have concerns about your account, please contact support@joymatcher.com"

### 8.2 Negative Review Management

**On Social Media:**
- Respond publicly (shows transparency)
- Apologize and offer to resolve
- Take detailed conversation to DM or email
- Never delete (unless abusive/spam)

**On Review Sites (Google, Trustpilot, App Store):**
- Monitor regularly
- Respond to all reviews (positive and negative)
- Thank positive reviewers
- Address negative reviews with empathy and solutions

**1. Sponsored Posts**
- Influencer creates content about JoyMatcher

- Links to JoyMatcher in bio

**2. Affiliate Program**
- Influencer receives unique referral link
- Earns commission for sign-ups or Premium upgrades
- Compensation: 10-20% per conversion

- Long-term partnership (6-12 months)
- Regular content (1-2x/month)
- Exclusive discount code for followers (use sparingly to protect premium positioning)
- Compensation: Monthly retainer + commission

## 1. Purpose & Philosophy

### 1.1 Why Testimonials Matter

Success stories are JoyMatcher's most powerful marketing asset. They demonstrate that our platform delivers on its core promise: **helping marriage-minded professionals find life partners**.

However, we handle testimonials differently than traditional dating apps:

- **Privacy-first**: We never publish without explicit, informed consent
- **Marriage-focused**: We highlight engagements, weddings, and long-term commitments—not casual dates
- **Premium positioning**: Stories reflect our sophisticated, serious user base
- **Cultural resonance**: We celebrate diaspora connections and cross-cultural relationships
- **Trust-building**: Authentic stories demonstrate platform effectiveness

### 1.2 Success Definition

For JoyMatcher, success means:
- **Engagements** (primary metric)
- **Marriages** (ultimate success)
- **Long-term committed relationships** (6+ months, explicitly marriage-oriented)

We do NOT feature:
- ❌ First dates or casual connections
- ❌ Short-term relationships
- ❌ Stories that don't align with marriage intent
- ❌ Stories that compromise user privacy

## 2. Consent Workflow

The system should detect potential success stories through:

1. **Account deactivation with reason "Found my person"**
2. **Relationship status update to "Engaged" or "Married"**
3. **User-initiated success story submission**
4. **VIP Concierge report of successful match**

### 2.3 Story Review Process

**Step 1: Initial Screening (Marketing Coordinator)**
- Review submission within 48 hours
- Verify user authenticity (cross-reference with platform records)
- Assess story quality and brand alignment
- Flag for approval or request additional details

**Step 2: Editorial Development (Content Manager)**
- Draft narrative from survey responses
- Create 3 versions:
  - **Short version** (150 words) - Social media, testimonial cards
  - **Medium version** (400 words) - Website testimonials, email
  - **Long version** (800-1200 words) - Blog features, press
- Select and edit photos (if provided)
- Ensure brand voice consistency

**Step 3: User Approval (Required)**
- Send draft versions to user via email
- Include all planned usage contexts
- Request explicit approval with signature/checkbox
- Allow edits and revisions (up to 2 rounds)
- Obtain final sign-off

**Approval Email Template:**

```
Subject: Your JoyMatcher Success Story - Review & Approval

Dear [Name],

Thank you for sharing your beautiful story! We've crafted the narrative below based on your responses. Please review carefully.

[DRAFT STORY - ALL VERSIONS]

**Where we plan to share this:**
• Website testimonials page
• Instagram and Facebook
• Email newsletter (March edition)
• [Other channels as specified]

**Your privacy settings:**
• Names: [First names only]
• Photos: [Included]
• Location: [City, Country]

**Next Steps:**
1. Review the story above
2. Request any changes (we'll revise up to 2 times)
3. Approve by clicking: [Approval Link]

**Important**: Once you approve, we may use this story across the channels listed. You can revoke consent anytime by emailing success@joymatcher.com.

Questions? Reply to this email.

Best regards,
The JoyMatcher Team
```

**Step 4: Legal Review (For High-Visibility Usage)**
- Press releases, paid advertising, or high-profile features require legal sign-off
- Ensure consent documentation is complete
- Verify no defamatory or legally risky content

**Step 5: Publication**
- Add story to testimonials database
- Tag by category (diaspora, VIP, engagement, wedding, etc.)
- Schedule publication across approved channels
- Track performance metrics

### 2.4 Ongoing Consent Management

**Users can revoke consent anytime by:**
- Emailing success@joymatcher.com
- Using dedicated "Manage My Story" link (sent in approval email)
- Contacting customer support

**Upon revocation request:**
- Remove story from website within 24 hours
- Remove from social media within 48 hours
- Add to "do not use" list
- Confirm removal to user via email

## 3. Story Categories & Publication Guidelines

### 3.1 Story Categories

**Category 1: Diaspora Connections**
- Highlights cross-border relationships
- Emphasizes cultural understanding
- Features home-abroad dynamics
- Showcases relocation journeys

**Example:**
> "Chioma, a marketing manager in Toronto, matched with Kwame, an entrepreneur in Lagos. Despite the distance, they connected over shared values, Nigerian heritage, and marriage intent. After six months of video calls and two visits, Kwame proposed. They're now planning a dual ceremony in Lagos and Toronto."

**Category 2: VIP Success Stories**
- Premium, high-touch service
- Professional, privacy-focused
- Concierge matchmaking emphasis
- Sophisticated positioning

**Example:**
> "As a busy physician with a demanding schedule, Dr. Amara had no time for endless browsing. JoyMatcher's VIP service introduced her to Chidi, a corporate lawyer who valued discretion and serious intent. Their expert-curated match led to engagement within eight months."

**Category 3: Engagement Announcements**
- Recent engagements
- Emotional, celebratory tone
- Focus on proposal stories
- Wedding date mentions (if shared)

**Example:**
> "Three months after matching on JoyMatcher, Tunde knew Adaeze was 'the one.' He proposed during a sunset dinner in Santorini—and she said yes! They credit JoyMatcher's progressive disclosure system for building deep trust before meeting."

**Category 4: Wedding Features**
- Married couples
- Wedding photos (if consented)
- Long-form blog features
- Inspirational, aspirational

**Example:**
> "From First Message to Forever: How Ngozi and Emeka Found Marriage on JoyMatcher. After failed attempts on casual dating apps, Ngozi joined JoyMatcher seeking serious commitment. She found Emeka—a software engineer who shared her faith, values, and vision for family. Eighteen months later, they married in a beautiful Lagos ceremony."

**Category 5: Cross-Cultural Stories**
- Different nationalities, religions, or backgrounds
- Highlights inclusivity
- Celebrates diversity within diaspora
- Shows platform breadth

**Example:**
> "Amina, a Muslim professional in London, and David, a Christian entrepreneur in Nairobi, found common ground through JoyMatcher's detailed compatibility tiers. Despite different faith backgrounds, they share values around family, respect, and commitment—and are now engaged."

### 3.2 Publication Standards

**Visual Standards:**
- High-resolution photos (minimum 1200px width)
- Professional or candid shots (no low-quality selfies)
- Diverse representation across stories
- Consistent color grading (warm, editorial, sophisticated)
- Overlay brand watermark (subtle, bottom corner)

**Narrative Standards:**
- **Tone**: Warm, sophisticated, aspirational (not overly cheerful or casual)
- **Length**:
  - Short: 100-200 words
  - Medium: 300-500 words
  - Long: 800-1500 words
- **Structure**: Hook → Background → JoyMatcher journey → Outcome → Advice
- **Voice**: Third-person for blog features, first-person for testimonials
- **Language**: Professional but accessible, culturally inclusive

**Required Elements in Every Story:**
1. How they found each other on JoyMatcher
2. At least one specific platform feature that helped (tier system, Show Interest, VIP service, etc.)
3. Timeline (matched → met → engaged/committed)
4. Current relationship status
5. One piece of advice for others

**Forbidden Elements:**
- ❌ Excessive detail about physical appearance or attraction
- ❌ Criticism of other dating platforms (focus on JoyMatcher positives)
- ❌ Financial details (salaries, wealth, gifts)
- ❌ Sexual content or innuendo
- ❌ Controversial political or social opinions
- ❌ Third-party names without consent (family, friends)

## 4. Social Media Sharing Guidelines

### 4.1 Platform-Specific Formats

**Instagram:**
- **Carousel posts** (3-5 slides)
  - Slide 1: Couple photo with overlay text ("From Match to Marriage")
  - Slide 2: Quote from story
  - Slide 3: Timeline graphic
  - Slide 4: Key platform feature highlight
  - Slide 5: Call-to-action
- **Reels** (30-60 seconds)
  - Story narration over photos/video
  - Trending audio (if appropriate)
  - Text overlays with key quotes
  - CTA: "Find your person. Link in bio."
- **Stories** (24-hour)
  - Quick highlights
  - Polls ("Would you relocate for love?")
  - Link stickers to full story

**Sample Caption:**
```
✨ From Toronto to Lagos: A JoyMatcher Love Story

Chioma and Kwame's story proves that distance is no match for genuine connection. After matching on JoyMatcher, they built trust through our progressive disclosure system—learning about each other's values, family goals, and life vision before meeting.

Six months of intentional communication led to an in-person meeting. Three months later, Kwame proposed. 💍

"JoyMatcher's tier system helped us go deep before going far," says Chioma. "We weren't just swiping—we were building."

Ready to find your person? Link in bio. 🔗

#JoyMatcherSuccess #DiasporaLove #MarriageMinded #NigerianDiaspora #LoveStory #Engaged #AfricanLove #FindYourPerson
```

**Facebook:**
- Longer narrative posts (300-500 words)
- Album posts with multiple photos
- Video testimonials (if available)
- Event tie-ins (Valentine's, wedding season)

**LinkedIn:**
- Professional angle: "How Serious Professionals Find Serious Partners"
- Career-focused narratives
- Executive/VIP success stories
- Thought leadership tie-ins
- Less frequent (1-2x/month max)

**X/Twitter:**
- Thread format (8-12 tweets)
- Quote highlights
- Engagement hooks

**Sample Thread:**
```

"No small talk. We went deep immediately," Chioma explains.

[Continue for 8-12 tweets total, ending with CTA]
```

### 4.2 Posting Frequency

**Recommended Schedule:**
- **Instagram**: 2-3 success stories/week (mix of posts, reels, stories)
- **Facebook**: 1-2 success stories/week
- **LinkedIn**: 1-2 success stories/month (highly curated)
- **X/Twitter**: 1 thread/week

**Seasonal Peaks:**
- January (New Year, fresh starts)
- February (Valentine's, engagement season)
- June-August (wedding season)
- November-December (holiday engagements)

### 4.3 Engagement & Response Guidelines

**When users comment on success stories:**

**Positive comments** ("Congratulations!" "This is beautiful!" "Goals!"):
- Like the comment
- Respond warmly: "Thank you! We're so proud of Chioma and Kwame. Ready to write your own story? 💜"

**Questions about the platform** ("How does this work?" "Is it available in my area?"):
- Respond promptly (within 2 hours during business hours)
- Provide helpful information
- Direct to website or DMs for more details

**Skeptical comments** ("This seems fake" "Are these real people?"):
- Respond professionally, not defensively
- "These are real members who consented to share their stories. We're proud of every couple who finds their person through JoyMatcher."
- Offer to answer specific questions via DM

**Negative comments** (criticism, trolling):
- Do NOT engage in arguments
- Delete if abusive, discriminatory, or violates community standards
- Respond once if legitimate criticism: "We appreciate your feedback and are always working to improve."
- Block/report if harassing

### 4.4 Paid Promotion Strategy

**When to boost/promote:**
- High-performing organic posts (500+ engagements)
- Featured VIP stories (showcase premium service)
- Seasonal campaigns (Valentine's, New Year)
- Launch periods (new markets, new features)

**Targeting:**
- Age: 28-45
- Location: Major diaspora cities (Toronto, London, New York, Atlanta, Houston) + home markets (Lagos, Accra, Nairobi)
- Interests: Professional development, African culture, marriage, relationships, faith communities
- Behaviors: Engaged shoppers, small business owners, frequent travelers

**Budget Allocation:**
- Test: $50-100 per story
- Boost top performers: $200-500 per story
- Major campaigns: $1,000-5,000 across multiple stories

## 7. Consent Documentation & Compliance

### 7.1 Required Documentation

For every published success story, maintain:
- **Initial survey response** (timestamped)
- **Drafted story versions** (all revisions)
- **User approval email** (with explicit consent)
- **Signed consent form** (for high-visibility usage)
- **Publication log** (where and when story was shared)
- **Revocation record** (if applicable)

### 7.2 Data Retention

- **Active stories**: Keep all documentation accessible
- **Revoked stories**: Archive documentation, mark "do not use"
- **Retention period**: 7 years after last publication (legal compliance)

### 7.3 GDPR/NDPR Compliance

**User Rights:**
- Right to access their story and documentation
- Right to request edits/corrections
- Right to revoke consent (remove story)
- Right to data deletion (after revocation, upon request)

**Process:**
- Respond to requests within 30 days
- Confirm identity before fulfilling requests
- Document all actions taken
- Notify user of completion

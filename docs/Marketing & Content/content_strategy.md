## 1. Purpose & Philosophy

### 1.1 Why Content Strategy Matters

Content strategy ensures every word we write—across website, blog, social media, email, and advertising—reinforces JoyMatcher's brand identity and resonates with our audience.

**Our content is NOT:**
- ❌ Casual or playful (like mainstream dating apps)
- ❌ Transactional or salesy
- ❌ Generic or interchangeable with competitors
- ❌ Focused on entertainment or virality

**Our content IS:**
- ✅ Sophisticated and purposeful
- ✅ Marriage-focused and intentional
- ✅ Culturally inclusive (especially African diaspora)
- ✅ Premium yet accessible
- ✅ Warm but serious
- ✅ Educational and trust-building

### 1.2 Content Mission

> **To speak authentically to marriage-minded professionals, honoring their values, culture, and time, while guiding them toward meaningful, lasting partnerships.**

## 2. Brand Voice

### 2.1 Voice Attributes

JoyMatcher's voice is defined by six core attributes:

**1. Sophisticated (not playful)**
- We speak to accomplished professionals, not casual daters
- We use refined, editorial language
- We avoid slang, emojis (unless culturally appropriate), and overly casual phrasing
- Example:
  - ❌ "Swipe right on your soulmate!"
  - ✅ "Find your life partner through intentional connection."

**2. Editorial (not gamified)**
- We position ourselves as a trusted advisor, not an entertainment platform
- We prioritize substance over clickbait
- We use thoughtful, well-researched content
- Example:
  - ❌ "10 Crazy Secrets to Make Him Propose!"
  - ✅ "Five Signs You're Ready for Marriage (And How to Know)"

**3. Warm (not cold or clinical)**
- We balance professionalism with empathy
- We acknowledge the vulnerability of relationship-building
- We celebrate success and support challenges
- Example:
  - ❌ "User #12345 has matched."
  - ✅ "Congratulations! You've taken a meaningful step toward finding your person."

**4. Respectful (not casual)**
- We honor the seriousness of marriage
- We respect cultural traditions and family values
- We never trivialize commitment
- Example:
  - ❌ "Let's get you hitched!"
  - ✅ "We're here to support your journey toward lifelong partnership."

**5. Inclusive (not exclusive)**
- We celebrate diverse backgrounds, especially African diaspora
- We speak to multiple nationalities, faiths, and life stages
- We avoid assumptions about users' circumstances
- Example:
  - ❌ "For Nigerian singles only"
  - ✅ "Celebrating African heritage while welcoming all marriage-minded professionals"

**6. Authoritative (not preachy)**
- We position ourselves as experts without being condescending
- We educate and inform, not lecture
- We back claims with research and real success stories
- Example:
  - ❌ "You MUST complete all tiers to succeed."
  - ✅ "Our research shows that completing higher tiers leads to better matches and stronger trust."

### 2.2 Tone Variations by Context

While our voice remains consistent, tone shifts based on context:

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Homepage | Aspirational, confident | "Find your life partner. Built for marriage-minded professionals." |
| Success Stories | Celebratory, warm | "From first message to forever: How Chioma and Kwame found love across borders." |
| Blog (Educational) | Thoughtful, advisory | "Navigating long-distance relationships requires intention, transparency, and trust." |
| Blog (Personal) | Reflective, empathetic | "Dating fatigue is real—and it's okay to feel discouraged sometimes." |
| Social Media | Engaging, conversational (still professional) | "What's your non-negotiable in a partner? For many of our members, it's shared values around family and faith." |
| Email (Onboarding) | Encouraging, supportive | "Welcome to JoyMatcher. Let's build your profile and start connecting with compatible matches." |
| Email (Retention) | Motivational, reassuring | "Finding the right person takes time. You're in the right place." |
| Error Messages | Apologetic, helpful | "Something went wrong. We're on it—please try again in a moment." |
| Legal/Policy | Clear, transparent | "Your privacy is our priority. Here's how we protect your data." |

  - Special focus on African diaspora

## 4. Writing Guidelines

### 4.1 Grammar & Style

**General Rules:**
- Use active voice ("We connect marriage-minded professionals" vs. "Marriage-minded professionals are connected")
- Avoid jargon unless explaining platform features (then define clearly)
- Use contractions sparingly (maintain sophistication but avoid stiffness)
- Write in second person for direct communication ("You'll connect with..." vs. "Users will connect...")
- Use serial comma (Oxford comma): "Trust, transparency, and intent"

**Capitalization:**
- Capitalize "JoyMatcher" and "VIP" always
- Tier names: Tier 1, Tier 2, etc. (capitalize "Tier")
- Features: "Show Interest," "Effective Disclosure Tier (EDT)"
- Lowercase: "diaspora" (unless part of a title)

**Numbers:**
- Spell out one through nine; use numerals for 10+
- Exception: Prices (₦18,000, $18), percentages (5%), statistics
- Use commas in large numbers: 10,000 not 10000

**Dates:**
- Format: Month Day, Year (e.g., February 26, 2026)
- Avoid abbreviations unless space-constrained

**Currency:**
- Use ₦ for Nigerian users (detected by IP or user settings)
- Use $ for all other users
- Display: ₦18,000 or $18 (include comma for thousands)

**Inclusivity:**
- Use "partner" or "life partner" (not "boyfriend/girlfriend" unless quoting)
- Use "they/their" for singular gender-neutral pronouns when appropriate
- Respect diverse faith backgrounds: "faith or belief orientation"
- Avoid assumptions about family structure (e.g., "introducing to family" not "introducing to parents")

### 4.2 Headline Writing

**Principles:**
- Clear over clever (avoid puns or wordplay that confuses)
- Benefit-focused (what's in it for the reader?)
- Specific and concrete (not vague)
- Active language (verbs, not nouns)

**Examples:**

**Homepage Headline:**
- ✅ "Find Your Life Partner. Built for Marriage-Minded Professionals."
- ❌ "Where Love Finds You" (too vague)
- ❌ "The Future of Dating" (not marriage-focused)

**Pricing Page Headline:**
- ✅ "Investment in Serious Commitment"
- ❌ "Affordable Plans" (undermines premium positioning)

**Blog Post Headline:**
- ✅ "Five Signs You're Ready for Marriage (And How to Know)"
- ❌ "Are You Ready?" (too vague)
- ❌ "Marriage Readiness 101" (too academic)

**Email Subject Line:**
- ✅ "Your Match Awaits: Complete Your Profile"
- ❌ "Don't Miss Out!" (too generic, salesy)

### 4.3 Calls-to-Action (CTAs)

**Primary CTAs (Conversion-Focused):**
- "Join JoyMatcher" (Homepage, blog)
- "Get Started" (Pricing, onboarding)
- "Upgrade to Premium" (In-app)
- "Apply for VIP" (VIP page)

**Secondary CTAs (Engagement-Focused):**
- "Learn More" (Features)
- "Read Success Stories" (Homepage)
- "Explore How It Works" (Homepage)
- "Browse the Blog" (Email)

**Microcopy CTAs (In-Context):**
- "Complete Your Profile" (Dashboard)
- "Show Interest" (Discovery)
- "Request Details" (Profile view)
- "Upgrade for Access" (Tier gate)

**Best Practices:**
- Use action verbs ("Join," "Explore," "Discover")
- Be specific about outcome ("Join JoyMatcher" not just "Sign Up")
- Create urgency without pressure ("Start Your Journey Today" not "LIMITED TIME ONLY!")
- Test variations (A/B test CTAs for optimization)

### 4.4 Storytelling Techniques

**For Success Stories:**
- Structure: Hook → Background → Challenge → JoyMatcher Solution → Outcome → Advice
- Use direct quotes from users
- Include specific details (names, locations, timelines)
- Highlight platform features that helped
- End with emotional payoff and CTA

**Example:**
```
When Chioma moved to Toronto for her MBA, she knew building a career would be demanding. But she didn't expect finding a life partner to be just as hard.

"I tried the mainstream apps," she says. "But no one seemed serious. It was all surface-level—no depth, no commitment."

Then she joined JoyMatcher. Within a week, she matched with Kwame, an entrepreneur in Lagos who shared her Nigerian heritage and marriage-focused intent.

"JoyMatcher's tier system meant we couldn't hide behind small talk," Chioma explains. "We discussed family goals, relocation, faith—everything that mattered—before we even met."

Six months later, Kwame proposed. They're now planning a dual ceremony in Lagos and Toronto.

Ready to find your person? Join JoyMatcher today.
```

**For Educational Content:**
- Structure: Problem → Context → Solution → Action Steps → Encouragement
- Use relatable scenarios
- Include research or expert insights
- Provide actionable takeaways
- End with reassurance and CTA

## 5. Content Types & Templates

**Homepage:**
- Hero section: Aspirational, benefit-focused
- Value propositions: Clear, concise (3-5 bullets)
- Social proof: Success stories, testimonials
- How it works: Step-by-step visual
- CTA: Prominent, repeated

**Feature Pages:**
- Headline: Benefit-driven
- Explanation: What it is, why it matters, how it works
- Visual: Diagram, screenshot, or illustration
- Use case: Real example
- CTA: Related to feature

- Headline: "Investment in Serious Commitment"
- Tier comparison table: Clear, scannable

## 7. Brand Lexicon

### 7.1 Preferred Terms

| Use This | Not This | Why |
|----------|----------|-----|
| Life partner | Soulmate | "Soulmate" is overly romantic/unrealistic |
| Marriage-minded | Looking for love | More specific and intentional |
| Progressive disclosure | Profile depth | Clearer about the system |
| Effective Disclosure Tier (EDT) | Compatibility score | Reflects our unique system |
| Show Interest | Like, swipe, match | More intentional, consent-driven |
| Tier completion | Profile progress | Emphasizes commitment |
| Verified identity | Real profiles | More professional |
| Consent-driven | Safe | More specific about what we do |
| Investment | Cost, price | Reframes as value, not expense |
| Curated matches | Recommendations | More premium positioning |
| VIP concierge service | VIP membership | Clarifies it's service, not browsing |
| Relationship infrastructure | Dating platform | Positions us as marriage-focused |

### 7.2 Forbidden Words/Phrases

**Never Use:**
- ❌ "Swipe" (too associated with casual apps)
- ❌ "Hook up" or "casual dating" (not our audience)
- ❌ "Hot," "sexy," "attractive" (too superficial)
- ❌ "Game," "play," "win" (not gamified)
- ❌ "Cheap," "affordable," "discount" (undermines premium positioning)
- ❌ "Easy," "fast," "instant" (marriage takes time)
- ❌ "Guaranteed" (we can't promise outcomes)
- ❌ "Singles in your area" (sounds like spam)

### 7.3 Cultural Sensitivity

**When Referencing African Diaspora:**
- ✅ "African diaspora professionals"
- ✅ "Nigerian heritage"
- ✅ "Cross-cultural connections"
- ❌ "Africans" (too broad, can be othering)
- ❌ "Back home" (assumes diaspora identity, not everyone relates)

**When Referencing Faith:**
- ✅ "Faith or belief orientation"
- ✅ "Spiritual values"
- ❌ "Religious" (can sound exclusionary)
- ❌ Assuming everyone has faith (offer "Agnostic" and "Atheist" options)

**When Referencing Family:**
- ✅ "Family values"
- ✅ "Parenting goals"
- ❌ "Starting a family" (assumes everyone wants kids)
- ❌ "Traditional family" (can alienate non-traditional structures)

## 8. Approval & Review Process

### 8.1 Content Review Tiers

**Tier 1: Self-Reviewed (No Approval Needed)**
- Social media captions (following guidelines)
- Blog post drafts (pre-publication)
- Email copy (following templates)
- In-app microcopy (standard notifications)

**Tier 2: Content Manager Review**
- Blog posts (before publication)
- Email campaigns (before scheduling)
- Landing pages (before launch)
- Success stories (narrative drafting)

**Tier 3: Marketing Lead Approval**
- Website homepage copy
- Major feature launches
- Pricing page updates
- Brand messaging changes

**Tier 4: Legal + Marketing Lead Approval**
- Terms of Service, Privacy Policy
- Press releases
- Paid advertising (high budget)
- Partnership announcements

### 8.2 Feedback & Revision Process

**Feedback Guidelines:**
- Be specific: "This headline is too vague" not "This doesn't work"
- Explain why: Reference brand voice or strategy
- Suggest alternatives: Provide examples
- Prioritize revisions: Major (must fix) vs. Minor (nice to have)

**Revision Timeline:**
- Minor edits: Same day
- Major rewrites: 2-3 days
- Legal review: 5-7 days

## 9. Quality Assurance Checklist

Before publishing any content, review against this checklist:

**Brand Voice:**
- [ ] Tone is sophisticated and professional (not casual or playful)
- [ ] Language is marriage-focused (not dating-focused)
- [ ] Content is respectful and inclusive

**Messaging:**
- [ ] Aligns with core messaging pillars
- [ ] Differentiates from competitors
- [ ] Addresses target audience pain points

**Clarity:**
- [ ] Headline clearly communicates benefit
- [ ] Copy is scannable (short paragraphs, bullets, headers)
- [ ] CTA is clear and action-oriented

**Accuracy:**
- [ ] All facts are verified
- [ ] Pricing is correct (₦ vs. $)
- [ ] Platform features are accurately described
- [ ] Links work and point to correct pages

**SEO (if applicable):**
- [ ] Primary keyword in title and headers
- [ ] Meta description optimized
- [ ] Alt text on all images

**Legal/Compliance:**
- [ ] No misleading claims
- [ ] User consent obtained (for testimonials)
- [ ] GDPR/NDPR compliant

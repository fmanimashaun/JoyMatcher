## Overview

VIP Coordination is the high-touch concierge service that differentiates JoyMatcher's premium offering. VIP Coordinators manage the entire VIP lifecycle: from application review to Tier 5 verification, expert assignment, quality monitoring, and success tracking. This document provides comprehensive procedures for VIP coordination.

---

## VIP Service Philosophy

### Core Principles

1. **White-Glove Service**
   - Every VIP receives personalized attention
   - Response time: <24 hours for all VIP inquiries
   - Proactive check-ins (not reactive support)
   - Treat VIPs as valued clients, not just users

2. **Quality Over Quantity**
   - Reject applicants who don't meet standards
   - Better to have 10 serious VIPs than 50 casual ones
   - Protect the integrity of the VIP pool
   - Expert assignments must be thoughtful, not rushed

3. **Trust Through Verification**
   - Tier 5 verification is non-negotiable
   - ID verification must be thorough
   - Video KYC confirms photo authenticity
   - Protect VIPs from each other (mutual verification)

4. **Expert Accountability**
   - Freelance experts work FOR VIPs, not for themselves
   - Performance is monitored continuously
   - Poor performers are replaced immediately
   - Client satisfaction is the ultimate metric

## Workflow 1: VIP Application Review

### Application Criteria

VIP applications are approved based on:
- **Seriousness of Intent** (40%): Clear marriage focus, realistic expectations
- **Profile Quality** (20%): Complete tiers, thoughtful responses
- **Compatibility** (20%): Fit with existing VIP pool
- **Communication** (10%): Professional, respectful language
- **Verification Potential** (10%): Likelihood of passing Tier 5 verification

4. What are your non-negotiable values in a partner?
5. How would you balance marriage with your career?

15. What do you expect from the VIP matchmaking service?

## Workflow 3B: Conflict-of-Interest Management

### Conflict-of-Interest Policy

**Critical Rule:** A VIP Expert may NOT handle two VIP clients who are interested in each other.

**Rationale:**
- Creates conflict of interest (expert represents both parties)
- Compromises expert objectivity
- Risks expert manipulating match for financial gain (paid per successful introduction)
- Violates trust-based matchmaking principles

---

### Identifying Conflicts of Interest

**Conflict occurs when:**
- Expert A is assigned to VIP Client #1
- Expert A is also assigned to VIP Client #2
- Client #1 and Client #2 are matched/introduced to each other

**How conflicts are detected:**
1. **Manual Detection:** VIP Coordinator reviews introduction proposals
2. **System Detection:** Automated check when expert creates introduction (future feature)
3. **Expert Self-Reporting:** Experts must disclose potential conflicts immediately

---

### Conflict Resolution Process

#### Step 1: Identify the Conflict

**Example Scenario:**
```
Expert: Chiamaka Nwosu (exp_003)
Assigned VIP Clients:
├─ Client A: Chidinma Okafor (female, 31, Lagos)
└─ Client B: Emmanuel Adeyemi (male, 34, Lagos)

Conflict Detected:
Expert Chiamaka creates introduction proposal:
"Chidinma and Emmanuel would be highly compatible..."

System Alert: ⚠️ CONFLICT OF INTEREST DETECTED
Both Client A and Client B are assigned to the same expert.
```

---

#### Step 2: Coordinator Intervention

**VIP Coordinator Actions:**
1. Immediately contact Expert Chiamaka
2. Confirm the conflict
3. Decide which client to reassign (typically the more recent assignment)
4. Select new expert for reassignment

**Email to Expert:**
```
Subject: Conflict of Interest - Client Reassignment Required

Hi Chiamaka,

We've identified a conflict of interest situation:
- You are assigned to both Chidinma Okafor and Emmanuel Adeyemi
- You proposed introducing them to each other

Decision: We will reassign Emmanuel Adeyemi to Expert Kemi Adeola (exp_001).

Kemi will handle Emmanuel's introduction to Chidinma.

Action Required:
```

#### Step 5: Expert Collaboration (Post-Handover)

**Once conflict is resolved, the two experts can collaborate:**

**Expert A (Chiamaka) and Expert B (Kemi) can now:**

**What experts CANNOT do:**
- ❌ Share confidential client notes without consent
- ❌ Pressure either client to accept the introduction
- ❌ Collude to manipulate the match outcome

---

### Conflict Prevention

**Preventive Measures:**
1. **Expert Load Balancing:** VIP Coordinator spreads VIP clients across multiple experts
2. **System Alerts:** Automated conflict detection when expert creates introduction
3. **Expert Training:** Experts trained to recognize and report conflicts immediately
4. **Regular Audits:** VIP Coordinator reviews expert assignments quarterly

---

### Multiple Conflicts (Rare Edge Case)

**Scenario:** Expert has 6 VIP clients, and 3 of them would be compatible matches for each other.

**Resolution:**
1. VIP Coordinator reassigns clients to minimize conflicts
2. Create expert "pods" (Expert A handles Client 1, Expert B handles Client 2, etc.)
3. Experts collaborate on introductions while maintaining separate client relationships

**Example:**
```
BEFORE (Conflict):
├─ Expert Chiamaka: Client A, Client B, Client C (all potentially compatible)

AFTER (Resolved):
├─ Expert Chiamaka: Client A
├─ Expert Kemi: Client B
└─ Expert Tolu: Client C

If Client A + Client B match:
├─ Expert Chiamaka coaches Client A
└─ Expert Kemi coaches Client B
```
